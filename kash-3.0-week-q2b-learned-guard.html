Week §7 · Q2b — Learned threshold: guarding against drift

Each chart spans ~12 weeks. Gray line = your typical daily load (creeping up). Black line = the learned over-commit threshold. Watch what the threshold does as your habits drift heavier.

You picked B · learned/adaptive. The threshold = your rolling typical day. If that baseline rises (more work crammed in over time), an unguarded threshold rises too — so chronic overload stops tripping any warning. For an ADHD wellbeing app, how should the learned line behave when your habits drift up?
B1 · Pure learned (no guard)

Line follows you, up or down

Threshold rises in lock-step with your habits.

  • Purest “normal for me”
  • Normalizes overload; never warns on slow burnout
B2 · Capped learned

Learns, but not above a ceiling

Personalizes downward freely, but flattens at a sane max — overload eventually trips it.

  • Personal + a wellbeing backstop
  • Simple rule
  • Ceiling is itself a (gentler) fixed number to pick
B3 · Learned + reflection flag

Adapts freely; reflection names the drift

Threshold stays fully learned, but the weekly/monthly Reflection guide (§11) surfaces a rising baseline: “your typical day has grown — intended?”

  • No arbitrary ceiling; respects autonomy
  • Catches burnout via reflection, not nags
  • Relies on the §11 reflection layer existing
B4 · Asymmetric (down fast, up slow)

Eager to lower, reluctant to raise

Drops quickly if you lighten up, but resists drifting upward — so creep still trips warnings for a long while.

  • Personal + built-in anti-creep bias
  • Subtle/opaque (“why didn’t it adjust?”)
  • Most logic to tune
